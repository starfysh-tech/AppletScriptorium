Patient-Reported Outcomes in Older Adults With Transthyretin Amyloid Cardiomyopathy - JACC

  • Patient-reported outcomes (PROs) play a crucial role in understanding the full patient experience living with transthyretin amyloid cardiomyopathy (ATTR-CM) and can help improve shared decision-making, treatment escalation, and assessment of treatment response.
  • In ATTR-CM trials, composite endpoints or "win" ratio approaches that include functional endpoints like 6-minute walk distance and PROs like KCCQ are used in combination with traditional cardiovascular outcomes to drive approval by the FDA.
  • Consistent slowing in the decline of HR-QoL has been observed in recent ATTR-CM trials, indicating the potential of PROs to monitor treatment effects and assess the impact of therapies on disease progression.

Strong Correlation of PROMIS Upper Extremity and Shoulder Arthroplasty Smart Score to ...

  • The study reveals strong correlations between PROMIS UE and SAS scores with the established ASES score in shoulder arthroplasty patients, suggesting these newer tools offer improved sensitivity for assessing upper extremity function.
  • PROMIS UE demonstrated excellent correlation at both 6 months (r =0.647) and minimum 2-year follow-up (r = 0.761) post-surgery with the ASES score, indicating its potential as an effective alternative measure.
  • Ceiling effect analysis indicated low ceiling effects for all three measures, including PROMIS UE and SAS, suggesting these newer instruments can assess a broad range of functional outcomes without reaching their maximum scores too quickly, enhancing their utility in clinical settings.

New Patient Outcome Assessed in Psoriasis Biologic Trial - Medscape

  • **Patient-Centered Outcome as Primary Endpoint:** The POSITIVE trial uses a patient-reported outcome, specifically the WHO-5 Well-Being Index, as the primary endpoint for evaluating recovery two years after initiating therapy with tildrakizumab in plaque psoriasis patients. This shift towards holistic care measures mental and physical recovery alongside disease control.
  • **Early Improvement in Mental Health:** Within 16 weeks of treatment initiation, patients showed a significant improvement in psychological well-being (WHO-5 score) from a baseline comparable to those with breast cancer or diabetes, indicating early positive effects on mental health despite similar skin clearance levels.
  • **Complex Relationship Between Physical and Mental Recovery:** Despite achieving clear skin, some patients experienced delays in improving their mental well-being as measured by the WHO-5 index. This suggests a complex interplay between physical disease control and mental recovery, potentially influenced by persistent anxiety about potential relapse.

Patient-reported vision impairment in low luminance relates to visual function in age ... - Nature

  • The Vision Impairment in Low Luminance (VILL) questionnaire effectively captures vision-related quality of life for individuals with AMD in low-luminance situations, focusing on challenges like reading and mobility.
  • Linear regression analysis shows a significant relationship between VILL subscale scores and visual function assessments including BCVA, LLVA, MA, and PR-CS, highlighting the relevance of these measures for assessing functional impacts of AMD.
